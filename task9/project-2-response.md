Project 1: Namiko's clusters

I found this project’s aesthetics really interesting and pleasing to my eyes. Namiko’s Clusters is a game that is based on the New York Times Connections game. When I first opened the game, I really enjoyed the aesthetics of it because it was quite different from the usual Connections games I played on the New York Times website.I especially enjoyed the wavy effect that the connection board has, and I appreciated the overall girly aesthetic with Kuromi, the Sanrio character, in the top-left corner. I think those design choices made the project feel more personal and playful. It was not just a copy of Connections, but a different version with its own mood and identity.

I also tried looking into the code because I was curious about how the audio was playing automatically without clicking, since browser permission for audio in HTML can be tricky. I was also curious about how she was able to achieve the toggle button along with dark mode. Unfortunately, I was not able to find the Phaser code, and when I inspected the browser, I was not able to see the code properly. It would be great to understand what happens behind the scenes code-wise, especially for the audio and dark mode.

Project 2: Yatsuki and Rayvhaun’s Project 2

I really enjoyed their project because this was something I attempted to do for my first assignment. I really liked how they imported draggable icons. It was really cute and made the website feel interactive in a nostalgic way.
I tried pressing the start button, but surprisingly it did not do much. I wondered if it was just a button that was there for a design purpose. Even if it was decorative, I think it still matched the overall interface. Their GIF engine had a cat button that generated a bunch of cat photos, which I appreciated a lot.
The drawing setup really reminded me of early Windows drawing applications. I was quite impressed by how they recreated the UI. It felt nostalgic but also creative. I also appreciated their selection of audio. I tried to create an audio player for my code as well, but it was not the easiest thing to do, so I appreciated seeing it working in their project.
I also enjoyed their little Photo Booth. I would have enjoyed it more if they had different filters, but I still thought it was a fun part of the project. Overall, I think they did a great job bringing out nostalgia in a creative way. It is diverse, cute, and creative.

Response 3: Escapism by Ben and Phil

As someone who has never done 3D before, this project was quite fascinating. I am not quite sure how three.js works, but I think Ben and Philippe did a great job recreating a world with a lot of flamingos flying around. It was quite fun to just move around and see what was there.
I enjoyed the birds flying around and how detailed they were. I was also impressed that this actually ran on Safari pretty smoothly, since a lot of 3D projects do not always run well on Safari. That made the experience easier and more enjoyable.
One thing I would appreciate is seeing the commands on the website, such as how to go up and down. At one point, I got confused while trying to move up and down, and I could not figure it out. I had to refresh the page just to see how it worked again. I think it would be helpful if the instructions stayed somewhere on the screen.
I also enjoyed the view from below and above. It reminded me of Minecraft when it is lagging, which made the experience feel strange yet fun. Overall, it was a fun project to experience.
